# Contributing to Awesome AI Governance

Thank you for considering a contribution.

## What belongs on the list

The list is for resources that materially help a practitioner build, ship, audit, or operate AI in a regulated environment. Specifically:

- Regulations, frameworks, and standards practitioners must comply with.
- Standards bodies and working groups where the technical conversation in this space happens.
- Research papers that have become reference points in industry practice (not just academic citations).
- Open-source primitives a regulated engineering team would actually use.
- Commercial governance platforms with mature regulator-facing posture.
- AI security and red-team tools, observability tools, audit tools.
- Regulators, policy-making bodies, and industry programmes.
- Conferences, newsletters, and books that practitioners actually read.

## What does not belong

- Pure AI ethics commentary, opinion essays, or philosophy reading lists. Other lists curate those.
- Generic AI news sites or aggregators.
- Tools or platforms that are early-stage and not yet usable in production.
- Self-promotion of products you have not used or do not work with.
- Affiliate links of any kind.
- Anything paywalled where the equivalent content is freely available elsewhere.

## PR shape

1. Open the PR against the relevant section. Keep the alphabetical order within each section if possible.
2. Use the format: `- [Name](URL) - short description. Why it matters.`
3. One sentence on what it is, one sentence on why a practitioner would care. No marketing language.
4. If you are the maintainer of the resource you are adding, disclose this in the PR description. It is fine to add your own; we just like to know.

## Style

- British English in the description copy.
- No em-dashes used as sentence punctuation.
- No emojis.
- Avoid banned vocabulary (delve, leverage, harness, robust, seamless, holistic, transformative, paradigm). Plain language wins.

## Review

A maintainer reviews PRs within seven days. Most PRs that meet the criteria above land within two weeks. PRs that need clarification stay open for up to thirty days; after that they close with a polite note.
